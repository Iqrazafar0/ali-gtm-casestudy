The Challenge

Most early-stage founders are stuck on warm intros. That's not a pipeline.

Warm intros are great for getting started. But they run out, they're unpredictable, and you can't scale them. At some point you need a system that brings in conversations you didn't already have a path to.

The real challenge is that cold outbound has a lot of moving parts and most founders don't have the time or background to figure all of them out between product sprints.

  • You know roughly who your ICP is, but you haven't turned that into a targeted, verified lead list.
  • Cold email infrastructure (domain setup, warmup, deliverability, sequences) is a real project on its own.
  • LinkedIn outreach done wrong gets accounts flagged. Done right, it's a meaningful second channel.
  • Without a CRM, interested leads fall through the cracks and follow-ups don't happen.

Healthcare & Dental Outreach

Health-tech advisory firm · US market · Event-based targeting
6 meetings
in 30 days
Company context

A health-tech advisory firm needed to reach decision-makers at companies attending healthcare and dental industry events across the US. They had no outbound system — prospecting was entirely manual and wasn't scaling.

Problem before working together

Their team was spending significant time manually identifying contacts at each event — a process that didn't scale and left most potential prospects untouched. There was no automated way to enrich contacts, validate emails, or run sequences at volume without the list going stale before anyone could reach out.

What was implemented
Exhibitor & attendee list scraping (12+ events) Apollo.io enrichment via n8n Bouncer email validation Event-contextual email sequences VP / Director / C-Suite targeting Apollo Sequences

Exhibitor and attendee lists were pulled from 12+ healthcare and dental conference websites, then run through Apollo's API for company enrichment and contact mapping at VP, Director, and C-Suite level. Every email was validated through Bouncer before entering any sequence. Sequences were kept short and event-specific — each one referenced the conference and a pain point relevant to that role. The whole workflow ran in n8n, meaning new events could be ingested automatically each month.

That specificity, combined with clean email validation, kept bounce rates low and reply rates healthy.

Legal Services Cold Outbound

YC-backed law firm · Series A–C startups · First cold pipeline
11 new clients
in 60 days
Company context

A law firm founded by Harvard Law graduates, backed by YC. Every single client they had came through YC referrals. The firm was solid but it had no way to grow outside the network it already had access to.

Problem before working together

Referral-only growth works until it doesn't. They had no cold pipeline, no outbound system, and no process for reaching founders they hadn't already met. They needed to start generating clients outside the YC circle for the first time — which meant building something from scratch.

What was implemented
ICP: Series A–C, FinTech / PropTech / HealthTech / Crypto Clay account filtering Apollo enrichment waterfall GPT-4 first-line personalisation 5-touch email sequence (14 days) LinkedIn connection flow (HeyReach) Apollo Sequences + CRM

Target accounts were filtered in Clay by funding date, headcount, and vertical — then cross-referenced to find companies with no in-house legal hires and no contract tooling in their stack. Those two signals pointed clearly to companies buying legal externally. GPT-4 wrote a personalised first line per contact referencing their funding round, product category, and a specific legal risk common to their vertical. Email ran 5 touches over 14 days in Apollo Sequences. LinkedIn ran a clean connection-first flow in HeyReach — no-note connection request, followed by a short message after acceptance, and a light follow-up if no response.

Results

Before this campaign, every single client had come through a referral. Within 60 days, 4 of the 11 new clients had no prior connection to the firm whatsoever — first time that had ever happened.

Key learning
What made it work The ICP filter was the most important decision. Instead of targeting "startups that might need legal help," we identified a specific structural signal, no in-house legal hire, no contract tooling, that pointed to companies actively buying legal services externally. Sharper targeting meant shorter sequences and faster conversions.
